\chapter*{摘\quad 要}\chaptermark{摘\quad 要}% 摘要标题
\setcounter{page}{1}% 开始页码
\pagenumbering{Roman}% 页码符号

本文研究一类动态目标抓取任务：机械臂在目标位姿和速度持续变化的条件下完成接近、相对对齐、夹爪闭合接触和目标搬运。模型预测控制（model predictive control, MPC）等基于模型的方法在状态观测准确时具有较强可解释性和约束处理能力，但在含噪声感知输入下容易将位姿抖动传递到参考轨迹和夹爪闭合判定。模仿学习能够从专家示范中学习动态追踪和阶段决策逻辑，但其闭环性能取决于数据表示、动作输出形式以及对观测噪声的鲁棒性。针对这一问题，本文在Genesis仿真平台中研究动态立方体抓取的以目标为中心的模仿学习方法，将抓取控制分解为高层参考生成与底层约束跟踪：学习策略预测任务空间相对动作块，共享MPC控制器负责约束求解与执行跟踪。该分层设计使BC、BC-RNN、ACT和Diffusion Policy（DP）可在同一MPC控制器下比较闭环性能。

本文首先定义包含平面平移和定轴旋转的动态抓取基准任务，并以有限状态机结合MPC的FSM-MPC算法专家生成合成示教数据。随后，本文将抓取过程表示为以目标为中心的历史相对观测、短时未来目标条件和相对任务空间动作块，使学习策略聚焦于相对位姿对齐和夹爪闭合时机，而由共享MPC控制器处理底层约束跟踪。基于该统一接口，本文评估行为克隆（BC）、循环行为克隆（BC-RNN）、动作分块Transformer（ACT）和Diffusion Policy（DP）四类模仿学习方法，并在真实观测、分离平动和转动的简化运动条件和含噪声观测条件下与FSM-MPC专家进行比较。实验结果显示，在真实完整场景的750回合评估中，四类学习策略的成功率集中在86.0\%--87.2\%的狭窄区间内，均能形成有效闭环控制；FSM-MPC专家达到97.1\%，体现了显式阶段逻辑在无噪声条件下的显著上限优势。含噪声评估进一步表明，四类模仿策略在高斯扰动和强组合噪声下均明显优于直接依赖原始噪声观测的未滤波FSM-MPC专家；但加入简单Kalman滤波后，FSM-MPC专家在仅含高斯扰动的中度噪声（Gaussian-only moderate）和完整中度噪声（Full moderate）下分别恢复到94.0\%和85.0\%成功率。上述结果表明，以目标为中心的表示、历史条件和动作块模仿可以在未显式建模状态估计时提供一定的时间平滑作用，但其优势依赖噪声类型和基线配置；对基于模型的方法基线而言，显式状态估计仍然至关重要。

\chapter*{Abstract}\chaptermark{Abstract}% 摘要标题

This thesis studies a dynamic grasping task where the manipulator must approach, align with, grasp, and transport an object while the target pose and velocity continuously change. Model predictive control (MPC) provides interpretable constraint-aware execution under accurate state feedback, but noisy perception can be directly propagated to reference motion and grasp timing. Imitation learning offers a complementary route by learning interception and phase-selection behavior from expert demonstrations, yet its closed-loop performance depends strongly on state representation, action parameterization, and robustness to observation noise. This thesis studies target-centric imitation learning for dynamic cube grasping in the Genesis simulation platform and compares deterministic and generative action-chunking policies under a shared MPC executor.

The proposed formulation represents each grasp phase using target-centric relative observation history, short-horizon target-motion conditions, and relative task-space action chunks. An FSM-MPC hand-crafted expert policy provides synthetic demonstrations, while the learned policy predicts grasp-stage references and the shared MPC executor handles low-level constrained tracking. Within this unified interface, behavior cloning (BC), recurrent behavior cloning (BC-RNN), Action Chunking Transformer (ACT), and a Diffusion Policy (DP) are evaluated under ground-truth, decomposed-motion, and noisy-observation settings. On the full ground-truth scene evaluated over 750 episodes, all four learned policies achieve success rates within a narrow 86.0\%--87.2\% band, while the FSM-MPC expert reaches 97.1\%, demonstrating a clear advantage of explicit phase logic when observations are accurate. Under noisy observations, learned action-chunking policies substantially outperform the unfiltered FSM-MPC expert under Gaussian jitter and strong combined noise; however, adding a simple Kalman filter restores the FSM-MPC expert's success rate to 94.0\% under Gaussian-only moderate noise and 85.0\% under full moderate noise. These results suggest that target-centric action-chunking imitation can provide temporal smoothing when no explicit estimator is used, but its advantage depends on the noise type and baseline configuration; explicit state estimation remains essential for a strong model-based baseline.
